[CrossTech announced](http://www.crossref.org/CrossTech/2009/10/recommendations_on_rss_feeds_f.html) that a [CrossRef](http://www.crossref.org/)
working group has written a [best practices](http://oxford.crossref.org/best_practice/rss/) for the use of RSS feeds by publishers. Nice introduction
for anyone who is creating RSS feeds. Only comment I could make, is the lack of other modules. For example, a Chemistry module has been proposed by
us 5 years ago already (DOI:[10.1021/ci034244p](http://dx.doi.org/10.1021/ci034244p)) and about which I blogged on
[several occasions](http://chem-bla-ics.blogspot.com/search?q=CMLRSS).

Below is the [CMLRSS feed](http://cb.openmolecules.net/atom.php?category=&type=latest_inchis) of [Chemical blogspace](http://cb.openmolecules.net/).

![](/assets/images/cmlrss_Cb2.png)

Of course, publishers can take advantage of such modules, using the [XML Namespaces](http://www.w3.org/TR/xml-names/) technology. The *best practices*
uses that for a [Dublin Core](http://dublincore.org/) and a [PRISM](http://purl.org/rss/1.0/modules/prism/) extension. The here discussed CML
extension is another one, but the point is, that you can basically plug in any module.
