I have been running automated curation tests for many years now, at least [from before 2018](https://chem-bla-ics.linkedchemistry.info/2018/10/11/two-presentations-at-wikipathways-2018.html).
Because it has been done without funding, it has not been as nicely integrated, and depends, for example, first on the RDF generation to be integrated
in the GitHub Action. So, I still run them regularly (often in the morning during breakfast). Meanwhile, the [curation tests](https://www.wikipathways.org/wikipathways-collection/index2)
help the project to monitor and maintain the quality of the pathways. The curation reports have been integrated into pathway pages for some
time now.

![](/assets/images/wpCurationBadge.png)

We have now integrated this curation badge into the author and community pages on the (not so) [new WikiPathways website](https://www.wikipathways.org/)
too. Authors can now find curation reports for pathways they started and also for the community pages:

![](/assets/images/4a0a20557574c3ae.png)

A second new feature is the "Citations" tab on both pages, which link to [Europe PMC](https://europepmc.org/)
with a dedicated search for articles mentioning those author or community pathways:

![](/assets/images/270716cef8d30481.png)

We hope you like it!
