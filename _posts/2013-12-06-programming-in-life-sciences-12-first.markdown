Yesterday was the last [Programming in the Life Sciences](http://chem-bla-ics.blogspot.nl/2013/10/programming-in-life-sciences-1-six-day.html) practical day,
and the 2nd and 3rd year B.Sc. [MSC](http://www.maastrichtuniversity.nl/web/Schools/MaastrichtScienceProgramme.htm) students presented their results yesterday
afternoon. I am impressed with the results that they reached in only six practical days. I have suggested them to upload the presentations to SlideShare or
[FigShare](http://figshare.com/) (with the advantage that you get a DOI), and asked them to send them their tools. Below are some screenshots.

The first app is by Tim and Taís, and look up activities from the [Open PHACTS](http://www.openphacts.org/) platform and filters it for activities related
to a set of five anti-oxidants (see also [their FigShare](http://figshare.com/articles/Tais_and_Tim_Final_Presentation_pptx/870474)):

![](/assets/images/timAndTais.png)


The next app is by Janneke and Lukas and uses the Open PHACTS [API](https://dev.openphacts.org/) to report on single protein targets for the compound
the user enters (see also [their SlideShare](http://www.slideshare.net/lukasfreedaheimfriedeheim/target-search-janneke-mes-lukas-friedeheim)):

![](/assets/images/jannekeAndLukas.png)

More apps will follow soon.
