Gang Fu and Evan Bolton have [blogged](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/rdf/) about it previously, but their PubChemRDF paper is out now
(doi:[10.1186/s13321-015-0084-4](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13321-015-0084-4)). It very likely defines the largest collection of RDF triples
using the [CHEMINF ontology](http://chem-bla-ics.blogspot.nl/search?q=CHEMINF&max-results=20&by-date=true) and I congratulate the
authors with a increasingly powerful [PubChem](http://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) database.

With this major provider of Linked Open Data for chemistry now published, I should soon see where
[my Isbjørn stands](http://chem-bla-ics.blogspot.nl/2012/07/isbjrn-4-added-cheminf-support.html). The release of this publication is
also very timely with respect to the CHEMINF ontology, as I last week finished a transition from Google to GitHub, by moving the important
wiki pages, including one about "[Where is the CHEMINF ontology used?](https://github.com/semanticchemistry/semanticchemistry/wiki/Where-is-the-CHEMINF-ontology-used%3F)".
I already added Gang's paper. A big thanks and congratulations to the PubChem team and my sincere thanks to have been able to contribute to this paper.
