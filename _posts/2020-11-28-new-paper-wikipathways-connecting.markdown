The last WikiPathways was already 3 years ago, an often used frequency for Nucleic Acids Research updates.
So, time for an update, and what an updates we had: *WikiPathways: connecting communities*
(doi:[10.1093/nar/gkaa1024](https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkaa1024)). This update focuses on the open,
collaborative nature of [WikiPathway](http://wikipathways.org/) and on the growing role of the portals,
like the [lipids portal](http://lipids.wikipathways.org/), the [AOP portal](http://aop.wikipathways.org/),
the [nanomaterials portal](http://nanomaterials.wikipathways.org/), and the
[inborn errors of metabolism (IEM) portal](http://iem.wikipathways.org/). There is also a lot happening
in the background, to make our tools better (much needed), our curation support better (in the future
available in multiple ways), our data model better, and our dissemination even better (e.g. with
Scholia/Toolforge and nanopublications). A huge thanks to [Marvin](https://orcid.org/0000-0003-2230-0840)
and [Tina](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7699-8191) to get everything together. Finally, if you haven't
recently checked the WikiPathways SPARQL endpoint, read [the paper](https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkaa1024) :)
